a brief on my being

I enjoy stopping to think about code mathematically and critically, and I am passionate about writing it! When I'm not tangled in the weeds of STEM, I enjoy writing satire, reading Dostoevsky, and listening: to people, post-punk, and philosophy podcasts.

Since May this year, I have been working with a group of scientists in the Applied Physics and Math department at Columbia, developing software to manipulate large databases, and researching an area of data science I had never before heard of - ‘spectral topological data analysis’. Naturally, there was a lot of learning on the job: navigating the initial struggles of learning Git, working in a team to brainstorm and build a full-stack web app in a month, and picking away at how I can use the data structures I learnt in class to speed up tedious grant burn computations. The combination of research, interaction, and creativity involved inspired my excitement for building software tools that impact people’s lives.

This excitement is propelled by my appreciation for the theoretical underpinnings of computer science – math! I first stumbled upon computational geometry in high school, and continued my research in the subject into college. Slicing and packing problems accelerate the gears in my brain. In addition, independently reading about and giving talks on probability theory taught me how to communicate technical things to an audience, a skill I will use as I design and give workshops on machine learning, UI/UX design, web development, and more to underrepresented students in tech with the non-profit Superposition. More generally, reading about topics in discrete math fuels my energy to make something concrete out of them – such as the gambler’s ruin game I am building now based on simple random walks!

Finally, for me, coding is not a purely objective, sequential process – but rather the act of contending with a living creature that demands my subjective interference. Developing products for people has challenged me to think critically about design and utility with my audience in mind, a mode of rigorous questioning is something I have internalised through over 8 years of debate, an activity I thoroughly enjoy!
